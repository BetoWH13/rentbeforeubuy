01

The tempting listing

It looks right. But does it fit your life?

A polished listing can show condition and features. It cannot show whether the platform works in your garage, commute, cargo routine, or household.

02

The rental reality check

Useful proxy. Imperfect specimen.

Rental fleets often mean ordinary trim, accumulated wear, and hard use. Treat the vehicle as a platform and category preview—not a verdict on the exact year or trim you may buy.

03

The method

Test the bones. Not the makeup.

Use the weekend to test the things that survive trim differences: seating position, visibility, controls, turning size, ride comfort, road noise, and everyday friction.

04

People fit

Bodies, seats, access, patience.

Install the actual child seat. Bring the passengers who matter. Check rear-door access, front-seat compromise, step-in height, sightlines, and whether anyone is already negotiating for mercy.

05

Cargo fit

Cargo volume is not cargo shape.

Load the stroller, crate, luggage, groceries, or work gear you actually carry. Check the opening, floor height, wheel-well intrusion, folded-seat shape, and hatch clearance.

06

Road fit

Drive the routes that will own your week.

Merge on your highway, cross your rough pavement, enter your garage, circle your school pickup, and park where spaces are mean and concrete columns hold grudges.

07

Parking fit

Big on paper becomes big every day.

Test mirror clearance, turning effort, camera usefulness, door opening room, curb visibility, and whether the vehicle fits without a small municipal planning meeting.

08

EV workflow

Range is a number. Charging is a routine.

For an EV, test cable reach, charger orientation, route planning, charging time, one-pedal driving, and whether your home and travel patterns make the workflow realistic.

09

Worst-case preview

Use the wear as evidence, not prophecy.

Fleet use can reveal rattles, worn controls, seat aging, loose trim, and suspension fatigue. Observe what hard use exposes—but do not assume one rental predicts every vehicle.

10

The decision

Promising fit. Exact-trim test. Daily-life mismatch.

The weekend should narrow the field, not pronounce a purchase verdict. Keep the platform if it fits your life, verify the exact trim separately, and inspect any specific used vehicle independently.
