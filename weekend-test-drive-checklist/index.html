The Weekend Platform Test

Weekend Test Drive Checklist Before Buying a Car

This is the core checklist from Drive Before Buy: a structured weekend test drive plan that turns a vague rental idea into a repeatable method. Use it to test seating comfort, cargo shape, parking fit, highway noise, night visibility, family and pet fit, EV charging routine, and the limits of what a rental can prove.

1. MatchBook the closest rental category to the car you are considering.
2. LiveDrive your commute, load your cargo, park in your real spaces.
3. DecideTurn notes into must-haves, deal-breakers, and next checks.

Start here

Book a weekend rental in the same category as the car you are considering. You probably will not get the exact trim, and that is fine. The point is to test the platform and category in your real life.

How to run the test well

Run the checklist across at least one full day that includes your real commute, your real errands, and your real passengers. A Sunday-morning loop on empty roads will not surface the annoyances that compound over years. A Saturday with school drop-off, grocery loading, and a parking-garage errand will.

After the rental, review your notes before you go back to shopping. The goal is to come out with three things: a confident answer on whether the category fits your life, a list of must-have features, and a list of deal-breakers that should disqualify specific trims.

Your checks and notes are stored only in this browser. Print or copy your notes before clearing.

What this checklist can and cannot tell you

It can help you test

Seating comfort, visibility, cargo space, parking size, highway noise, commute feel, infotainment annoyance, family or pet fit, and EV charging routine when you can rent an EV.

It cannot prove

Hidden damage, title status, maintenance quality, future repair costs, exact used-EV battery health, or exact trim feel when the rental is different from the car you may buy.

FAQ

Can I save my checks and come back later?
Yes — your checks and notes are saved in this browser only, automatically, as you type. They do not leave your device. Use the "Print this checklist" button to make a paper copy you control. Use "Clear saved checks & notes" to start over.
Will my saved data sync to my phone?
No. The data is local to this browser. If you want it on another device, print it or copy the notes into a note app.
Do I have to do every item?
No. Skip sections that do not apply (for example, the EV section if you are testing a gas car, or the car-seat section if you do not have kids). The checklist is a menu of observations, not a mandate.
What if I cannot rent the exact car I want to buy?
That is normal. Use a category rental — see how to find a similar rental. The platform test still works even if the trim is different.
